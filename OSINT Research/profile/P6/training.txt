Today I visited the Colosseo restoration site again to study the new structural reinforcement techniques. The engineering team has done remarkable work balancing modern seismic requirements with the original Roman concrete composition. As a restoration architect, I find this intersection of ancient and modern engineering endlessly fascinating.

The challenge with historical structures is always the same: how do you strengthen without altering the character? Every intervention must be reversible, minimal, and respectful of the original intent. The Italian Soprintendenza enforces these principles rigorously, which sometimes makes projects longer but ultimately preserves authenticity.

I presented my research on Baroque facade conservation at the Milano conference last month. The reception was encouraging. Several colleagues from Vienna and Barcelona expressed interest in collaborative projects. Cross-border restoration methodology is an area with significant potential for knowledge exchange.

[REDDIT - r/architecture - u/arch_fontana_roma]

One question that comes up frequently in restoration work is how to handle previous interventions that were themselves historically significant but technically inadequate. At Palazzo Farnese, for example, some 19th-century repairs used Portland cement that is now causing damage to the original travertine. Removing these repairs is itself a form of historical erasure, but leaving them accelerates deterioration.

The decision framework we use involves documenting everything thoroughly before any intervention, then consulting with the Soprintendenza on a case-by-case basis. There is no universal rule, which is both the beauty and the frustration of this work.

# ArchiCAD Heritage Tools v0.3
Custom GDL objects and scripts for historical building documentation in ArchiCAD. Designed specifically for Italian heritage restoration projects compliant with Soprintendenza requirements.

## Features
- Parametric GDL objects for classical architectural elements (columns, cornices, capitals)
- Decay mapping templates for stone and plaster surfaces
- Integration with photogrammetric survey data
- Report generation for Soprintendenza documentation

Developed for personal use in my restoration practice. Contributions welcome from fellow heritage architects.

[RESEARCHGATE - Maria_Fontana - abstract]

Conservation of Baroque Facades in Seismic Zones: A Methodological Framework for Minimal Intervention. This paper proposes an integrated approach to structural assessment and conservation planning for historical facades in earthquake-prone regions, with specific application to 17th-century buildings in central Rome. The methodology combines traditional visual inspection with modern non-destructive testing, photogrammetric documentation, and finite element analysis.
